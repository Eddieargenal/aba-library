# Summary

This ALNAP guide by Chloe Campbell argues that humanitarian actors must understand cities as complex interconnected systems — not just as locations for delivering services — before designing response programs. It introduces a systems framing for urban humanitarian response that addresses infrastructure, markets, governance, land tenure, services, and social networks as interdependent components. The guide is foundational for urban ABA appropriateness decisions, particularly the urban systems interdependence test.
