# 03 — Frameworks

This section contains two tiers of frameworks. **Tier 1** frameworks are operational decision frameworks — each one answers a specific atomic decision question at a defined lifecycle stage, has explicit decision logic (scoring tables or branching rules), and drives one or more tools. **Tier 2** frameworks are reference frameworks — structured analytical references, checklists, and design tools that support but do not replace the Tier 1 decision chain.

Use Tier 1 frameworks when you need to make a defensible programme decision. Use Tier 2 frameworks when you need reference architecture, analytical depth, or sector-specific design support.

---

## Tier 1 — Operational Decision Frameworks

Nine frameworks covering every major decision point in the ABA/DRR programme lifecycle, from appropriateness determination through to handover. Each framework has: an atomic decision question, a structured decision logic (table or branching model), red-flag override conditions, failure mode analysis, concept anchoring, tool instantiation, and source foundation.

### 1. [[2017-aba-appropriateness-decision-framework]] — Stage 0: Appropriateness
**What:** Evidence-based framework defining 9 decision domains and a weighted scoring model (0–100) to determine whether an area-based approach is needed, feasible, and justified for a given crisis context.
**Why:** Without this gate, programmes adopt ABA design for operational convenience rather than evidence of geographic concentration and multi-sector interdependence — producing coordination complexity with no added value.
**When/How:** Apply before any area-level programme design begins; drives Tool #01 (ABA Feasibility and Necessity Assessment); score ≥50 is the prerequisite for all subsequent lifecycle frameworks.

### 2. [[2017-area-selection-framework]] — Stage 1: Area Selection
**What:** Weighted 5-domain scoring framework (severity, DRR relevance, access, leverage, boundary definability) for comparing and prioritizing candidate areas for ABA programming, with a 4-tier priority output.
**Why:** Selection driven by donor geography or operational convenience rather than severity and risk reaches the most legible areas, not the most vulnerable ones, and cannot be reversed once programming begins.
**When/How:** Apply after appropriateness is confirmed (score ≥50) and before in-depth diagnosis; drives Tool #02 (Area Selection Matrix) and Tool #03 (Boundary Definition Tool); areas scoring ≥55 proceed to in-depth diagnosis.

### 3. [[2017-neighborhood-diagnosis-framework]] — Stage 2: Neighbourhood Diagnosis
**What:** Completeness threshold model across 5 diagnostic domains (urban systems, HEVC, service functionality, stakeholder landscape, community dynamics) with minimum evidence standards per domain.
**Why:** Without systematic multi-domain diagnosis, programme design replicates prior sector templates regardless of local conditions and misses the system-level leverage points that distinguish ABA from co-located sector response.
**When/How:** Apply immediately after area and boundary confirmation; drives Tool #04 (Urban Systems Diagnosis), Tool #05 (HEVC Risk Mapping), and Tool #06 (Stakeholder Mapping); all 5 domains must meet minimum evidence standard before strategy design begins.

### 4. [[2026-area-based-coordination-framework]] — Stage 3: Coordination Architecture
**What:** Two-axis branching framework (host-government engagement level × actor density) for selecting the appropriate area coordination structure and governance architecture, including TOR requirements and dissolution criteria.
**Why:** Area-based programming does not automatically produce area-based coordination; without deliberate architecture, a platform defaults to a reporting loop rather than a decision-making body that resolves gaps and duplication.
**When/How:** Apply after stakeholder landscape is complete from stage 2; drives Tool #06 (Stakeholder Mapping) for axis inputs and Tool #13 (Coordination Dashboard) for ongoing platform management.

### 5. [[2017-joint-prioritization-framework]] — Stage 4: Joint Prioritization
**What:** Weighted 6-domain scoring framework (risk severity, vulnerability, systems interdependence, DRR relevance, feasibility, actor responsibility) with a Basics First override condition for life-safety issues.
**Why:** Without a joint cross-sector process, sector leads default to their own domain priorities, producing parallel sector programmes rather than an integrated area response — systematically underweighting issues no single actor owns.
**When/How:** Apply in a cross-sector joint session once diagnosis is complete and coordination platform is functioning; drives Tool #08 (Joint Risk and Needs Prioritization Matrix); community validation is required before outputs are finalized.

### 6. [[2017-integrated-area-strategy-framework]] — Stage 5: Strategy Integration
**What:** Six-domain readiness gate model (theory of change coherence, sector integration, local resource leverage, DRR alignment, coordination fit, phasing) for approving an area strategy before implementation begins.
**Why:** Without an explicit integration step, diagnosis and prioritization produce sector programmes in the same geography, not an area response — coordination grafted on afterward cannot replace strategy-level integration.
**When/How:** Apply after prioritization outputs are agreed and coordination platform is established; drives Tool #10 (Integrated Area Strategy Builder) and Tool #09 (Response Option Comparison); all six domains must score ≥2 for approval.

### 7. [[2019-implementation-adaptation-framework]] — Stage 7: Implementation Adaptation
**What:** Three-tier adaptation classification table (minor adjustment, significant modification, fundamental redesign) with differentiated approval authority, evidence thresholds, timelines, and documentation requirements.
**Why:** Urban ABA programmes operate in dynamic environments where without predefined adaptation criteria any change can be framed as adaptation — eroding accountability with communities and donors while allowing programme drift.
**When/How:** Apply throughout implementation whenever a programme change is proposed; drives Tool #12 (Sequencing and Dependency Map) for dependency assessment and Tool #16 (M&E Adaptation Framework) for evidence generation.

### 8. [[2017-transition-handover-framework]] — Stage 10: Transition and Handover
**What:** Five-gate binary readiness model (handover readiness, receiving actor capacity, resource transfer completeness, residual risk management, exit timeline) for determining when and how to transfer area ownership to local or municipal actors.
**Why:** When handover is treated as a close-out event, local actors receive responsibilities without resources, community gains reverse within months, and the programme leaves no sustainable local capacity.
**When/How:** Design handover criteria at programme inception; apply gates progressively from mid-implementation; drives Tool #17 (Handover and Scale-Up Checklist); partial handover of components that pass gates is preferred over full delay.

### 9. [[2015-urban-drr-response-design-framework]] — Cross-cutting: DRR Design
**What:** Two-stage framework: a HEVC completeness gate (blocks strategy design until hazard and exposure minimums are met) and a Sendai priority alignment quality check (4-dimension scoring applied at strategy approval).
**Why:** Urban DRR response fails when designed from sector mandates without risk analysis (increasing future exposure) or from technical risk maps without community validation (producing maps communities reject).
**When/How:** Apply Stage 1 at the end of neighbourhood diagnosis (stage 2) before strategy design begins; apply Stage 2 at strategy approval (stage 5); drives Tool #04, Tool #05, and Tool #10.

## Tier 2 — Reference Frameworks

Supporting frameworks providing analytical depth, sector-specific design reference, and checklist tools. These do not replace the Tier 1 decision chain but provide structured reference architecture at specific decision points.

### DRR and Resilience Foundations

- [[2007-community-based-drr-design-tables-framework]] — Twigg-derived design tables for community-based DRR programming; reference for domain-level DRR indicator design.
- [[2007-community-resilience-characteristics-tables-framework]] — Tables mapping resilience characteristics across five Thematic Areas; reference for resilience indicator selection.
- [[2007-community-resilience-indicator-set-framework]] — Indicator set for measuring community resilience; reference for M&E design in DRR programmes.

### Urban Resilience Investment and Screening

- [[2013-city-resilience-investment-logic-framework]] — World Bank investment logic for city resilience; no-regret and flexible measure typology; cost-benefit reference for DRR infrastructure decisions.
- [[2013-risk-based-city-neighborhood-screening-framework]] — Screening methodology for city and neighbourhood-level risk-based prioritization; reference for multi-area triage.

### Build Back Better and HEVC Reference

- [[2015-build-back-better-recovery-alignment-tool-framework]] — Alignment tool for ensuring recovery investments meet BBB standards; reference for post-disaster reconstruction quality checks.
- [[2015-hazard-exposure-vulnerability-capacity-mapping-framework]] — Detailed HEVC mapping framework; reference for designing the full HEVC data collection methodology.

### Urban Systems and Context Analysis

- [[2016-urban-systems-mapping-framework]] — Campbell-derived framework for mapping urban systems across five typologies (physical, economic, social, institutional, environmental); reference for domain 1 of neighbourhood diagnosis.
- [[2017-urban-context-analysis-framework]] — IRC/IIED 10-step urban context analysis framework; reference for structuring the full diagnosis process.
- [[2017-urban-response-analysis-framework-uraf]] — Mohiddin et al. Urban Response Analysis Framework; reference for multi-system response analysis and sector integration.
- [[2017-urban-resilience-action-design-framework]] — Design reference for resilience-oriented urban programming; reference for strategy domains 3 and 4.
- [[2017-urban-service-gap-analysis-framework]] — Framework for mapping and scoring service gaps across urban systems; reference for domain 3 of neighbourhood diagnosis.

### Assessment and Planning Tools

- [[2017-area-based-approach-suitability-framework]] — Suitability criteria for applying area-based approaches in different urban crisis types; reference for appropriateness decision.
- [[2017-participatory-assessment-and-planning-tool-framework]] — Structured participatory assessment and planning methodology; reference for community engagement design in stages 2–5.
- [[2017-stakeholder-and-power-mapping-framework]] — Power-sensitive stakeholder mapping methodology; reference for domain 4 of neighbourhood diagnosis and coordination architecture design.
- [[2017-local-market-and-service-provider-mapping-framework]] — Framework for mapping local markets and service providers; reference for domain 3 (local resource leverage) in the integrated area strategy.
- [[2017-severity-risk-feasibility-prioritization-matrix-framework]] — Matrix for scoring risk severity, feasibility, and actor responsibility; reference for joint prioritization methodology.
- [[2017-response-option-comparison-matrix-framework]] — Comparison matrix for evaluating response modality options; reference for sector integration in strategy design.
- [[2017-protection-and-do-no-harm-risk-screen-framework]] — Protection risk screening framework for ABA programme design; reference for red-flag assessment at each lifecycle stage.

### Municipal Integration

- [[2017-municipal-integration-plan-framework]] — Framework for developing a municipal integration plan that connects area-level ABA programming to city-level planning systems.
- [[2017-municipal-drr-action-checklist-ten-essentials-framework]] — UNDRR Ten Essentials checklist adapted for municipal DRR action planning; reference for enabling environment assessment.
- [[2017-municipal-ten-essentials-diagnostic-framework]] — Diagnostic framework for assessing municipal DRR capacity against the Ten Essentials; reference for government engagement in stage 3 coordination design.

### Theory of Change and M&E

- [[2017-area-theory-of-change-results-framework]] — Results framework and theory of change structure for area-level ABA programming; reference for domain 1 of the integrated area strategy.
- [[2017-aba-monitoring-and-evaluation-framework]] — M&E framework for area-based programmes; reference for adaptation trigger design in the implementation adaptation framework.

### Transition and Handover Reference

- [[2017-handover-readiness-checklist-framework]] — Checklist framework for assessing handover readiness against capacity, resource transfer, and residual risk criteria; companion reference to the Transition and Handover Framework.

### Assessment and Design Tools (2019)

- [[2019-area-based-assessment-with-key-informants-framework]] — REACH/UNHCR key informant methodology for area-based assessment; reference for data collection design in neighbourhood diagnosis.
- [[2019-area-based-reassessment-tool-framework]] — Tool framework for structured reassessment of area conditions at programme mid-point; reference for the diagnosis reassessment trigger.
- [[2019-replication-scale-up-learning-note-framework]] — Learning note framework for documenting the conditions enabling replication and scale-up; reference for the scale-up trigger in the transition and handover framework.
- [[2019-sector-technical-design-checklist-framework]] — Sector-specific technical design checklist; reference for quality-checking sector components of an integrated area strategy.
- [[2019-sequencing-and-dependency-map-framework]] — Framework for mapping sequencing logic and technical dependencies across programme activities; reference for phasing design in strategy and adaptation.
- [[2019-settlement-based-intervention-package-design-framework]] — Design framework for settlement-level intervention packages; reference for sub-area targeting within an ABA programme.
- [[2019-settlement-neighborhood-delineation-framework]] — Framework for delineating settlement and neighbourhood boundaries using community, administrative, and risk geography inputs; companion to the area selection boundary definition process.
