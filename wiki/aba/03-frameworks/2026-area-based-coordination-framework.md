# Area-Based Coordination Framework

## Atomic question
Given the actor landscape in this area, what coordination architecture is needed and how should it be structured?

## Why this decision matters
Area-based programming does not automatically yield area-based coordination — this must be deliberately designed (DFID/IRC/ALNAP 2015). Without deliberate architecture, a coordination platform defaults to a reporting loop where actors share updates rather than a decision-making body that resolves gaps and duplication. Getting the architecture wrong — particularly creating parallel systems to existing municipal structures — undermines sustainability and erodes host-government ownership of the response.

## Prerequisites
- From stage 1 (Area Selection): confirmed operational area and boundary
- From stage 2 (Neighbourhood Diagnosis): completed stakeholder and actor landscape mapping
- Minimum evidence standard: actor 4W/5W data covering the full area; at least one direct contact with municipal authority to establish their engagement level and willingness

## Decision domains

1. **Coordination mandate and scope** — what sectors, actors, and geographic area does the platform cover? The mandate must be defined upfront and agreed by all members. Scope should not be expanded informally as the programme grows.

2. **Relationship to cluster/sector coordination** — how does the area platform interface with existing national or city-level cluster and sector mechanisms? Area platform decisions must feed upward to clusters; cluster decisions must inform the area platform. These are complementary, not competing levels.

3. **Host-government and municipal interface** — is local government leading, co-leading, observing, or absent? The IASC principle is government-led or at minimum government-coordinated. Each engagement level requires a different platform structure and a different sustainability plan.

4. **Convenor role and decision authority** — who calls meetings, sets the agenda, records decisions, and tracks follow-through? The convenor must have the mandate and perceived neutrality to challenge duplication or fill gaps across actors. This role cannot be assigned to the actor with the largest budget if that actor has conflicting interests.

5. **Accountability and information flows** — how are platform decisions recorded, shared with non-members and relevant cluster leads, and converted into assigned actions with named responsible parties and timelines?

## Decision logic

Apply a two-axis branching model to select the appropriate coordination structure:

**Axis 1 — Host-government engagement level:**

| Government posture | Coordination structure |
|---|---|
| Active and willing | Government-led platform; humanitarian actors in technical support roles; formalize with signed TOR |
| Willing but limited capacity | Co-led platform with explicit capacity transfer plan; humanitarian actor supports administration, government chairs |
| Absent or not yet engaged | Humanitarian-led interim platform; define government handover conditions upfront in TOR; build in quarterly re-engagement check |
| Obstructive | Humanitarian-led with formal notification protocol to government; document the obstruction and escalate through HCT |

**Axis 2 — Actor density in the area:**

| Actor count | Coordination mechanism |
|---|---|
| >10 actors present | Formal coordination structure: agreed TOR, regular scheduled meetings, mandatory decision log, gap/duplication tracker |
| 3–10 actors | Light mechanism: shared information protocol, periodic coordination calls, no formal TOR required but agreed information-sharing norms |
| <3 actors | Bilateral coordination only; no platform needed; revisit at 3-month mark as actor presence evolves |

**Combined decision rule:**
- Government-led + >10 actors → full formal Area-Based Coordination (ABC) platform with TOR
- Government-led + <3 actors → bilateral with government notification protocol; formal platform not warranted
- Humanitarian-led + >10 actors → formal humanitarian-led platform with explicit government handover conditions in TOR
- Humanitarian-led + <3 actors → bilateral coordination only; no platform infrastructure

Per IASC Standard TOR 2026: ABC is a subnational, operational, multi-sectoral mechanism — NOT a replacement for cluster coordination. Establishment requires: concentrated multi-sector needs, a coordination gap that existing mechanisms do not fill, and an actor with capacity and mandate to chair. Dissolution criteria must be set at establishment, not at exit.

## Red flags / override conditions

- Platform design creates a parallel system to existing municipal coordination structures → redesign to interface with, not duplicate, existing bodies; presenting to municipal body is not the same as duplicating it
- No actor in the area is willing to accept the convenor role → do not launch a platform; use bilateral coordination until a convenor with legitimate mandate is identified
- TOR has not been agreed by at least the three largest actors in the area before launch → delay launch; a platform with an unagreed TOR has no legitimacy to make binding decisions or challenge non-compliance
- Cluster leads have not been informed of the platform scope and relationship before launch → inform all relevant cluster leads before first meeting to prevent conflicting sector coordination signals reaching implementing actors

## Failure modes

- Breaks when the platform becomes a reporting mechanism (actors share updates) rather than a decision-making body (actors resolve gaps and duplication) — platform meeting agendas must include items that require decisions, not only information sharing
- Breaks when area platform decisions are not communicated to cluster leads, creating contradictory sector guidance at area and national level simultaneously
- Breaks when the convenor role is assigned to the actor with the largest budget or staff presence rather than the actor with the most appropriate mandate and perceived neutrality — legitimacy depends on neutrality, not funding share
- Breaks when no dissolution or transition criteria are written into the TOR at establishment — platforms that outlast their purpose consume coordination energy without adding value and resist closure even when programming has ended
