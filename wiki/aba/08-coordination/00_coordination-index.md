# 08 — Coordination

Structures, tools, and protocols for multi-actor area-based coordination and information management. Use when designing coordination architecture, managing 4W/5W data, or establishing inter-agency referral and decision-making protocols. Three template files are fully populated; the rest are stubs awaiting extraction from source documents.

## Documents

| Document | Description |
|---|---|
| [[actor-responsibility-matrix]] | **What:** A matrix template clarifying which actor leads, supports, monitors, and coordinates each activity domain in the area. **Why:** Prevents accountability gaps when multiple organizations operate in the same location. **When/How:** Complete after initial 5W mapping; review at each coordination cycle; flag domains with no lead actor assigned. |
| [[coordination-meeting-brief-template]] | **What:** A standardized template for area coordination meeting briefs covering agenda, attendance, decisions, action points, and gap/duplication reporting. **Why:** Ensures institutional memory and accountability across actor turnover. **When/How:** Use for every area coordination meeting; number meetings sequentially and archive all briefs. |
| [[decision-log-template]] | **What:** A template for logging coordination decisions with rationale, responsible actor, deadline, and status. **Why:** Provides accountability and memory for decisions made across coordination meetings. **When/How:** Number decisions sequentially within the area; review open decisions at every meeting; flag items overdue by more than 2 weeks. |
| [[4w-5w-management]] | **What:** Guidance on collecting, managing, and using 5W (Who/What/Where/When/Whom) data for area coordination. **Why:** Enables gap detection, duplication prevention, and area strategy monitoring. **When/How:** Maintain continuously; update at each coordination cycle and cross-reference with actor-duplicated activity data. |
| [[area-based-coordination-model]] | **What:** Defines how humanitarian actors, local authorities, and service providers coordinate within a defined geographic area. **Why:** Prevents duplication and fills service gaps across overlapping actor interventions. **When/How:** Use to orient new actors and review when response scope changes or new actors arrive. |
| [[area-platform-setup]] | **What:** Step-by-step guidance for establishing a coordination platform from scratch. **Why:** Ensures proper convening, terms of reference, data-sharing protocols, and actor relationship management. **When/How:** Use at the start of any new area-based response or when standing up a new coordination node. |
| [[cbo-local-actor-engagement]] | **What:** How to identify, engage, and integrate CBOs and community groups into ABA coordination. **Why:** Builds local leadership, addresses capacity gaps, and transitions coordination toward local actors. **When/How:** Begin during initial stakeholder mapping; maintain through regular engagement reviews. |
| [[cluster-sector-interface]] | **What:** How area coordination platforms interact with the cluster system. **Why:** Ensures proper reporting, sector standards alignment, and avoidance of duplicating cluster functions. **When/How:** Review when new sectors activate or area platform scope changes. |
| [[cluster-vs-area-coordination]] | **What:** Clarifies the relationship between cluster/sector mechanisms and area-based coordination. **Why:** Prevents confusion over authority, scope, and decision-making between parallel coordination structures. **When/How:** Use during response design and when new actors question which coordination body has authority. |
| [[coordination-architecture]] | **What:** How to design the overall coordination architecture for an area-based program. **Why:** Establishes clear authority, membership, meeting structure, and cluster relationships from the start. **When/How:** Apply during initial response planning; revise as response scales or transitions. |
| [[coordination-data-sharing]] | **What:** How to establish data sharing protocols between coordination actors. **Why:** Manages data sensitivity, consent, security, and GDPR/protection compliance across shared platforms. **When/How:** Draft before platform launch; review whenever new data types or actors join. |
| [[duplication-detection]] | **What:** Methods and systems for detecting and preventing activity duplication across actors. **Why:** Maximizes resource efficiency and prevents beneficiaries from being double-counted or over-served in one zone. **When/How:** Run continuously via 5W tracking; investigate overlaps flagged at coordination meetings. |
| [[gap-analysis]] | **What:** Methods for systematically identifying unmet needs or uncovered geographic zones. **Why:** Ensures no population or location falls through the cracks in area response planning. **When/How:** Conduct at regular coordination cycles; assign actors to priority gaps identified; track closure. |
| [[information-management]] | **What:** How to establish and maintain information management systems for area coordination. **Why:** Provides the data backbone (5W, dashboards, gap maps, reports) that all other coordination functions depend on. **When/How:** Set up at platform launch; maintain continuously with regular data validation checks. |
| [[local-resource-leverage]] | **What:** Guidance on identifying and integrating local actors, institutions, markets, and community resources into coordination. **Why:** Improves outcomes and sustainability by leveraging existing local capacity rather than creating parallel systems. **When/How:** Map during initial assessment; integrate into planning and referral pathways continuously. |
| [[municipal-alignment]] | **What:** How to align area-based programs with municipal government priorities, systems, and DRR plans. **Why:** Ensures long-term sustainability and avoids creating parallel governance structures. **When/How:** Begin at response design; review at each planning cycle for alignment with municipal updates. |
| [[municipal-engagement]] | **What:** How to engage municipal authorities as coordination partners. **Why:** Defines roles, builds relationships, manages political risks, and aligns coordination with existing government systems. **When/How:** Start at inception; escalate issues through coordination meetings when municipal engagement breaks down. |
| [[referral-pathway-design]] | **What:** How to design functional referral pathways between actors and sectors. **Why:** Ensures clear referral triggers, handoff protocols, tracking, and feedback loops for beneficiaries. **When/How:** Design during initial coordination setup; review when new actors or sectors join the platform. |
| [[referral-pathways]] | **What:** Guidance on establishing, communicating, and managing referral pathways to ensure multi-need beneficiaries are tracked to service completion. **Why:** Prevents beneficiaries from being lost between actors when they have needs spanning multiple sectors. **When/How:** Communicate at onboarding of new actors; review breakdowns at coordination meetings; update pathways quarterly. |
