# Transect Walk Observation Form

## Purpose
A structured observation log for a systematic transect walk through a defined area to document urban systems status, infrastructure conditions, social conditions, service access, and spatial hazard patterns along a pre-defined route. More detailed than the rapid area observation form.

## When to use
Stage 2 (area profile and systems diagnosis), after area selection is complete.

## Who collects / facilitates
Field team of 2-3, including at minimum one staff with urban systems knowledge. Bilingual, including community guide if possible.

## Respondents / participants
Observation only; brief informal conversations with residents encountered along route are encouraged but not required.

## Enumerator / facilitator guidance
Plan the transect route in advance on a map. Include both high-need zones and apparently functional zones. Record GPS waypoints at each observation. Spend minimum 15 minutes per 500m stretch.

## Data quality checks
- Observations by 2 staff should be compared and reconciled before submission
- Any section covering < 50% of planned route should be flagged as partial
- Cross-check section E hazard ratings with secondary hazard data (INFORM, FloodMap, etc.)

## Analysis use
Populate urban systems status matrix in Tool #04. Identify leverage points and critical failures. Feed HEVC evidence into Tool #05.

## Risks / ethical safeguards
- Do not record personal data about individuals observed
- Be cautious in areas with security actor presence
- Conduct in daylight only unless security conditions allow otherwise
