# 05 — Field Instruments

18 data collection instruments including observation forms, KII guides, checklists, mapping forms, and decision templates used in field assessments. 15 instruments are fully populated and ready to use; duplication-gap-analysis-matrix, household-mini-survey, and participatory-mapping-guide are stubs.

## Documents

| Document | Description |
|---|---|
| [[rapid-area-observation-form]] | **What:** A structured form for rapid visual assessment of a candidate area documenting physical conditions, service functionality, hazard markers, and population density. **Why:** Provides the first systematic evidence pass before committing to detailed assessment — usable in 1–2 field days. **When:** Stage 0 or early Stage 1, before Tool 01 evidence collection begins. **How:** Walk the candidate area using the form section by section; use findings to populate Tool 01 geographic concentration and multi-sector need domains. |
| [[transect-walk-observation-form]] | **What:** A structured observation log for a systematic transect walk documenting urban systems, infrastructure, social conditions, service access, and spatial hazard patterns along a defined route. **Why:** More thorough than the rapid observation form — captures infrastructure and systems interdependency detail not visible from a spot survey. **When:** Stage 2 (area profile and systems diagnosis), after area selection is confirmed. **How:** Field team of 2–3 walks a pre-defined route, completing each section by observation and brief informal conversation; consolidate findings into the service-functionality-mapping-sheet. |
| [[hazard-exposure-vulnerability-capacity-matrix]] | **What:** A structured HEVC analysis matrix following the Sendai Framework risk formula (Risk = Hazard × Exposure × Vulnerability / Capacity) for the target area. **Why:** Produces the risk profile needed for DRR integration in the area strategy and identifies priority risk reduction measures. **When:** Stage 2 (area profile), after completing observation and KIIs but before finalizing area strategy. **How:** Field officer with DRR knowledge synthesizes data from observation and secondary sources; validate hazard and vulnerability sections with community before finalizing. |
| [[kii-guide-municipality]] | **What:** A KII guide for municipal authorities and local government officials covering governance capacity, local plans, resource allocation, coordination willingness, and political context. **Why:** Collects institutional and political context critical for assessing ABA feasibility and potential for local government partnership. **When:** Stage 0 (appropriateness decision), as part of Tool 01 Modules 5 and 9. **How:** Conduct a 60–90 minute semi-structured interview with a senior municipal official; use the guide as a prompt not a script; record verbatim quotes for key positions. |
| [[kii-guide-service-provider]] | **What:** A KII guide for service providers — utility companies, water authorities, health facility managers, school directors, and market associations. **Why:** Collects service functionality, capacity constraints, repair plans, and coordination potential that underlies the urban systems diagnosis. **When:** Stage 0–2, during evidence collection for Tool 01 and the urban systems diagnosis. **How:** Conduct interviews per service type; compile findings into the service-functionality-mapping-sheet for cross-sector comparison. |
| [[kii-guide-community-leaders]] | **What:** A KII guide for community leaders — block leaders, neighbourhood associations, elders, and women's group leaders. **Why:** Collects community-level knowledge about needs, vulnerabilities, social dynamics, and participation feasibility that institutional sources cannot provide. **When:** Stage 0 (appropriateness decision) and Stage 3 (community engagement), required for participation feasibility assessment in Tool 01 Domain 8. **How:** Interview diverse leaders ensuring gender and minority representation; use findings to complete the participation-feasibility-checklist. |
| [[kii-guide-market-actors]] | **What:** A KII guide for market actors — traders, market association leaders, commodity suppliers, and landlords. **Why:** Collects market functionality, supply chain status, price levels, and cash transfer feasibility needed for the economic dimension of the area strategy. **When:** Stage 0–2, when assessing urban systems interdependence and local resource leverage. **How:** Interview 2–3 key market actors per commodity type; triangulate findings against rapid observation data and secondary market information. |
| [[kii-guide-ngos-cbos]] | **What:** A KII guide for NGOs and CBOs operating in the target area covering activities, coverage, coordination experience, local capacity, and duplication. **Why:** Maps the actor landscape needed for coordination complexity scoring and prevents duplicating existing local capacity. **When:** Stage 0 (stakeholder mapping), as part of Tool 01 Module 5 (coordination complexity). **How:** Interview representatives from all major NGOs and CBOs in the area; compile into the stakeholder-5w-mapping-form. |
| [[kii-guide-protection-actors]] | **What:** A KII guide for protection actors — child protection organizations, GBV service providers, displacement monitoring units, and legal aid organizations. **Why:** Identifies protection risks, do-no-harm constraints, and safe participation feasibility that must be understood before ABA design. **When:** Stage 0, required before finalizing the participation feasibility assessment. **How:** Conduct a confidential interview; flag any protection risks in the decision memo and use findings to complete the participation-feasibility-checklist. |
| [[service-functionality-mapping-sheet]] | **What:** A structured matrix for mapping pre-crisis and current functionality of all major urban services in the target area. **Why:** Produces the service functionality baseline that quantifies urban systems failure for the area profile and strategy design. **When:** Stage 2 (area profile), after KIIs with service providers and before finalizing the systems diagnosis. **How:** One row per service type; complete pre-crisis and current status columns from KII and observation findings; identify functional gaps for the area strategy. |
| [[stakeholder-5w-mapping-form]] | **What:** A structured 5W (Who, What, Where, When, for Whom) form for cataloging all actors, their activities, coverage, target populations, and timelines in the area. **Why:** Feeds directly into coordination gap and duplication analysis — identifies where coordination overhead is justified and where actors are absent. **When:** Stage 2 (stakeholder mapping) and ongoing during implementation for coordination updates. **How:** One row per actor; complete from KII findings and secondary data; update monthly during implementation to track changes. |
| [[local-resource-inventory]] | **What:** A structured inventory of local resources — human, material, institutional, financial, and social — within the target area. **Why:** Prevents external actors from duplicating what local systems can provide; grounds the area strategy in existing capacity. **When:** Stage 2 (area profile) and Stage 5 (integrated area strategy design). **How:** Compile from KII findings across all actor types; categorize by resource type; cross-reference against identified needs to map leverage points. |
| [[participation-feasibility-checklist]] | **What:** A structured checklist assessing whether meaningful community participation is feasible — covering safety, inclusion, timeline, language, accessibility, and institutional mechanisms. **Why:** Participation is a defining criterion of ABA; this checklist makes the feasibility decision evidence-based rather than assumed. **When:** Stage 0 (appropriateness decision), before finalizing the ABA decision. **How:** Complete each item using evidence from KIIs with community leaders and protection actors; use findings to score Domain 8 in Tool 01. |
| [[operational-feasibility-checklist]] | **What:** A structured checklist assessing operational feasibility of implementing an ABA program — covering access, staffing, time, data quality, systems capacity, protection safeguards, and exit planning. **Why:** Operationalizes Domain 9 scoring in Tool 01; prevents committing to ABA in contexts where implementation is not actually feasible. **When:** Stage 0 (appropriateness decision), before finalizing the ABA decision. **How:** Complete each item against organizational capacity and field context; use findings to score Domain 9 in Tool 01. |
| [[decision-memo-template]] | **What:** A structured memo template for documenting the ABA appropriateness decision — summarizing assessment evidence, Tool 01 scoring, red flags, and the final recommendation. **Why:** The required output of Tool 01 and the formal accountability record for the ABA design choice. **When:** End of Stage 0, after completing all Tool 01 modules. **How:** Complete each section sequentially from Tool 01 output; obtain program manager sign-off before proceeding to Stage 1. |
| [[household-mini-survey]] | Brief 15-20 minute household survey for a non-random sample in the target area — capturing needs, assets, priorities, and service access to complement qualitative data. |
| [[duplication-gap-analysis-matrix]] | Matrix for identifying where actors provide the same service or where no actor operates in a given area — guiding coordination and resource allocation decisions. |
| [[participatory-mapping-guide]] | Facilitation guide for community participatory mapping sessions — community members identify hazards, assets, service gaps, and priority zones on area maps. |
