# Kii Guide Service Provider

## Purpose
A key informant interview guide for service providers — utility companies, water authorities, health facility managers, school directors, market associations. Collects data on service functionality, capacity constraints, repair plans, and coordination potential.

## When to use
Stage 2 (area profile and systems diagnosis). After completing observation and before finalizing urban systems diagnosis.

## Who collects / facilitates
Field officer with urban systems knowledge. Technical staff preferred for infrastructure-heavy interviews.

## Respondents / participants
Water utility district manager; electricity service manager; health facility in-charge; school head teacher; market association representative. Aim for at least 3 different service types.

## Questions / interview guide

### Consent and introduction
- Introduce organization and assessment purpose
- Confirm use of information (internal planning only)
- Duration: 30-45 minutes

### A: Service status
1. What is the current operational status of [service] in [target area]? What % of normal capacity?
2. What was the pre-crisis status of [service] in this area?
3. What are the main causes of current service disruption or reduction?
4. Which zones within [target area] are most affected by service disruption?

### B: Repair and restoration plans
5. Is there a repair or restoration plan for [service] in this area? What is the timeline?
6. What resources are needed to restore service? What is the estimated cost?
7. Are there any barriers to restoration beyond resources — technical, political, access?
8. What would accelerate the restoration of [service] in this area?

### C: Population coverage and equity
9. Which population groups are currently NOT receiving [service] in this area? Why?
10. Are there informal or community-led service alternatives operating in the area? What is their coverage and quality?
11. Are there any groups being excluded from [service] access due to non-payment, documentation, or discrimination?

### D: Coordination
12. Are you coordinating with any humanitarian organizations on service delivery in this area? With whom?
13. Have any organizations offered support for service restoration? What kind? Has it been useful?
14. What kind of support from external organizations would be most useful?
15. Are you in contact with the municipal disaster management authority about this area?

### E: Data and documentation
16. Do you have any spatial data, maps, or reports showing service coverage in this area? Can you share?
17. Do you track the number of people served vs. pre-crisis baseline? Can you share data?

## Enumerator / facilitator guidance
Request any service coverage maps or functionality data before or after the interview. Follow up in writing for data sharing. If utility companies are unresponsive, try ward-level or community water committee contacts.

## Data quality checks
- Must cover minimum 3 different service types to constitute strong evidence for Domain 4
- Cross-check service provider claims with observation and community interview data
- Note discrepancies between official status and observed/community-reported status

## Analysis use
Populate service functionality matrix in Tool #04 (urban systems diagnosis). Feed into Domain 3 (multi-sector needs) and Domain 4 (urban systems) scoring in Tool #01.

## Risks / ethical safeguards
- Service providers may underreport failures due to accountability concerns
- Be alert to political sensitivities around infrastructure failures
- Do not share community complaints about specific providers without anonymization
