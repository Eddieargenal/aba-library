# Kii Guide Community Leaders

## Purpose
A key informant interview guide for community leaders — block leaders, neighborhood associations, community elders, women's group leaders. Collects community-level knowledge about needs, vulnerabilities, social dynamics, local capacities, and participation feasibility.

## When to use
Stage 0-2 (appropriateness through systems diagnosis). Must include women leaders and marginalized group representatives, not only formal male leaders.

## Who collects / facilitates
Field officer with community facilitation skills. Female staff required for interviews with female community leaders. Language skills essential.

## Respondents / participants
Formal community/block leader; women's group representative; youth group representative; representative of a marginalized group (minority, IDP). Minimum 3 different types of community leaders, with at least 1 female and 1 representing a marginalized group.

## Questions / interview guide

**Interviewer note:** Conduct separate interviews for different types of leaders. Do NOT conduct with formal male leader present when interviewing female or marginalized group leaders.

### Consent and introduction
- Introduce organization and purpose
- Confirm voluntary participation and no obligation
- Duration: 30-45 minutes

### A: Community knowledge — needs and conditions
1. What are the most serious problems facing residents of this area right now?
2. Which parts of the area (zones, streets, blocks) have the most serious needs?
3. Which households or groups are in the most difficult situation? Who is being left behind?
4. What has changed most in this area since the crisis?

### B: Community capacities and resources
5. What are the main assets and resources available in this community — organizations, skills, informal systems, mutual aid?
6. What community groups or associations are active here? What do they do?
7. Are there community members who have relevant technical skills (construction, health, water management)?
8. Are there community-managed services or infrastructure (communal water points, community security, collective waste management)?

### C: Social dynamics and power
9. Are there tensions between different groups in the community (newcomers/established residents, ethnic/religious groups, IDPs/host community)?
10. Are there community members who are excluded from community decision-making — women, youth, specific ethnic groups, persons with disability?
11. Who do most residents turn to when they have problems? Whose voice carries most weight in this community?

### D: Participation feasibility
12. Have there been community consultations or meetings about the response so far? Who organized them? Who participated?
13. Are there safe spaces for women to participate in community decision-making here?
14. What would it take for all community members — including marginalized groups — to genuinely participate in program design?
15. Are there any cultural or security constraints that would affect participation?

### E: Response and coordination
16. Which organizations are currently working in this area? What are they doing?
17. Are there any organizations whose activities are causing problems or creating tensions?
18. What do residents want from organizations working here?
19. Has your community group been consulted by any organization? Was it meaningful or tokenistic?

## Enumerator / facilitator guidance
Do not interview formal leaders only — this will underrepresent women, youth, IDPs, and marginalized groups. Separate interviews reduce social pressure. Ask for referrals to leaders of groups you haven't reached.

## Data quality checks
- Must include at least 1 female leader and 1 marginalized group representative
- Cross-check community power analysis with protection actors' assessment
- Flag any significant discrepancy between leader accounts and household survey findings

## Analysis use
Populate Domain 6 (local capacity) and Domain 8 (participation feasibility) in Tool #01. Identify community structures for engagement platform design in Tool #07.

## Risks / ethical safeguards
- Be aware that formal leaders may overstate community cohesion or their own representativeness
- Protect identity of informants if they raise concerns about powerful local actors
- Do not share information about community tensions with authorities without consent
