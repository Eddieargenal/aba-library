# Kii Guide Market Actors

## Purpose
A key informant interview guide for market actors — traders, market association leaders, commodity suppliers, landlords. Collects data on market functionality, supply chain status, price levels, and cash transfer feasibility in the target area.

## When to use
Stage 2 (area profile and systems diagnosis) and Stage 6 (modality selection). Essential before making cash transfer decisions.

## Who collects / facilitates
Field officer with market systems knowledge. Livelihoods specialist if available.

## Respondents / participants
Market association representative; major commodity traders (2-3); supply chain representative; local landlord/rental market actor. Minimum 3 different actor types.

## Questions / interview guide

### Consent and introduction
- Brief purpose introduction; emphasize no commercial obligation
- Duration: 20-30 minutes

### A: Market functionality
1. What goods and services are currently available in [target area] markets?
2. What goods are unavailable or in short supply that were available before the crisis?
3. How have supply chains been affected? What is causing disruptions?
4. Are goods available in neighboring areas or markets accessible from [target area]?

### B: Price levels
5. How have prices changed for key commodities (food, shelter materials, water) since the crisis?
6. What is driving price increases — supply shortage, demand spike, speculation?
7. Are price levels stable or still changing?

### C: Cash transfer feasibility
8. If affected households received cash, would they be able to purchase what they need in local markets?
9. Are mobile money or digital payment systems functional in this area?
10. Would large-scale cash distributions affect market prices or create problems for the market?

### D: Rental market
11. What is the current rental market situation — availability, prices, conditions?
12. Has the crisis affected rental prices? By how much?
13. Are landlords willing to rent to displaced households, IDPs, or households with housing subsidies?

### E: Recovery and investment
14. Are traders and businesses planning to reopen, expand, or relocate? What is driving decisions?
15. What support would most help the local market recover?

## Enumerator / facilitator guidance
Interview small traders as well as large actors — their perspectives differ significantly. Cross-check price information across multiple traders. Ask to see stock records if available.

## Data quality checks
- Minimum 3 different market actor types to constitute strong evidence
- Cross-check price claims with at least 1 additional source (secondary data or household survey)
- Note if market actors have conflicts of interest in their responses

## Analysis use
Populate market functionality section of urban systems diagnosis (Tool #04). Inform modality selection (Tool #09) — cash vs. in-kind decision.

## Risks / ethical safeguards
- Market actors may provide self-serving information about cash transfers
- Do not share trader-specific pricing information competitively
- Be alert to speculation or artificial scarcity
