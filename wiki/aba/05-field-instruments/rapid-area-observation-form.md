# Rapid Area Observation Form

## Purpose
A structured field observation form for rapid visual assessment of a candidate area — documenting physical conditions, service functionality, hazard markers, infrastructure status, and population density patterns. Used in the first 1-2 days of field work before detailed data collection.

## When to use
Stage 0 (appropriateness decision) and Stage 1 (area selection). First instrument used in any ABA assessment.

## Who collects / facilitates
Field officer with context knowledge; bilingual preferred; minimum 2 staff for safety.

## Respondents / participants
No direct respondents — field observation only. Supplement with brief informal conversations with community members if safe.

## Enumerator / facilitator guidance
Walk at least 2 transects through the area — one along main roads, one through interior. Document GPS track. Do not enter areas with active security incidents. Note all areas NOT reached.

## Data quality checks
- Cross-check observation findings with at least 1 secondary data source before using for scoring
- Flag any Section not covered (due to access limits) as weak evidence
- Two observers should conduct independent observations in separate zones if area is large

## Analysis use
Synthesize observations to score Domain 2 (geographic concentration), Domain 3 (multi-sector needs), and Domain 4 (urban systems) in Tool #01. Flag any red flags observed.

## Risks / ethical safeguards
- Do not record household-level beneficiary data on this form
- Do not photograph individuals without consent
- Store securely; contains location data
- Do not share GPS coordinates with security actors without authorization
