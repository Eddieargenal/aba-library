# Kii Guide Protection Actors

## Purpose
A key informant interview guide for protection actors — child protection organizations, GBV service providers, displacement monitoring units, legal aid organizations. Collects data on protection risks, do-no-harm constraints, and safe participation feasibility in the target area.

## When to use
Stage 0-1 (appropriateness decision). Must be completed before finalizing area selection and before designing community consultation mechanisms.

## Who collects / facilitates
Protection-trained staff. Female staff required for GBV-focused interviews. Strict confidentiality protocol.

## Respondents / participants
Child protection organization field officer; GBV service provider coordinator; displacement/IDP monitoring organization; UNHCR protection officer if present. Minimum 2 different protection actor types.

## Questions / interview guide

**Confidentiality note:** This interview does not collect case-level data. All information is used for program design context only.

### Consent and introduction
- Strict confidentiality assurance — no attribution, no case details
- Duration: 40-50 minutes

### A: Protection context
1. What are the main protection concerns in [target area]?
2. Which population groups face heightened protection risks (women, children, IDPs, ethnic minorities, LGBTQ+)?
3. Are there areas within [target area] where protection risks are particularly elevated?
4. Have there been reported incidents of GBV, child abuse, exploitation, or forced displacement related to the crisis?

### B: Do-no-harm for area-based programming
5. Are there targeting methods that would put beneficiaries at risk in this area (e.g., public lists, visible distributions)?
6. Would geographic area-based targeting create or exacerbate tensions between groups?
7. Are there community actors who might exploit a coordination platform for gatekeeping or exclusion?
8. What data collection methods would be inappropriate or unsafe in this area?

### C: Participation safeguards
9. Are there safe spaces for women and girls to participate in community meetings?
10. What participation mechanisms are culturally safe for marginalized groups in this area?
11. Are there community or traditional actors whose involvement in consultations could silence vulnerable groups?

### D: Referral pathways
12. What protection services currently exist in or accessible from [target area]?
13. Are there functional referral pathways between humanitarian actors and protection services?
14. What are the main gaps in the protection referral system?

### E: Red flags
15. Are there any actors — formal or informal — operating in this area that pose protection risks?
16. Are there any planned forced evictions, resettlement, or military operations that would affect [target area]?

## Enumerator / facilitator guidance
Do not push for case-specific information. Focus on systemic and contextual issues only. Protection actors may be reluctant to share — respect their confidentiality obligations. Use findings to inform design, not for attribution.

## Data quality checks
- Minimum 2 independent protection actor sources required to constitute strong protection evidence
- Any single protection red flag from a reliable source should be treated as a hard red flag regardless of score

## Analysis use
Populate Domain 8 (participation feasibility) and Domain 9 (operational feasibility) in Tool #01. Document any red flags that could override positive scores.

## Risks / ethical safeguards
- Strict information security required — protection actor information must not be shared outside program design team
- Do not store case-specific information even if shared
- Do not share protection findings with authorities without explicit consent from protection actors
