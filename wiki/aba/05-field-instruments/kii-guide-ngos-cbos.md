# Kii Guide Ngos Cbos

## Purpose
A key informant interview guide for NGOs and community-based organizations (CBOs) operating in the target area. Collects data on actor activities, geographic coverage, coordination experience, local capacity, gaps, and duplication in the area.

## When to use
Stage 0-2 (appropriateness decision through stakeholder mapping). Essential for 5W/actor mapping.

## Who collects / facilitates
Field officer or coordinator. Should have sector knowledge relevant to the organization being interviewed.

## Respondents / participants
Program manager or field coordinator of NGO/CBO with activities in target area. Target minimum 5 different organizations including at least 2 local CBOs.

## Questions / interview guide

### Consent and introduction
- Introduce organization and purpose of coordination assessment
- Duration: 30-40 minutes

### A: Actor profile and activities
1. What activities is your organization implementing in [target area]? In which zones?
2. How many households/individuals are you currently reaching? Target population?
3. Since when have you been operating in this area?
4. What are your plans — scale-up, scale-down, exit, continuation?
5. Do you have a geographic map of your coverage? Can you share it?

### B: Coordination
6. Are you participating in any coordination mechanisms for this area — cluster, working group, ad hoc coordination?
7. Have you experienced duplication of activities with other actors in this area?
8. Are there significant gaps — needs not being addressed by any actor that you are aware of?
9. Would you be willing to participate in an area-level coordination platform?
10. What would make area coordination valuable and not just an administrative burden?

### C: Local capacity
11. Are there local CBOs or community groups in this area that have capacity to implement or co-implement response activities?
12. Are you currently partnering with or sub-granting to local organizations in this area?
13. What are the main capacity gaps of local organizations here?

### D: Data and knowledge
14. Have you conducted any needs assessments in this area? Can you share the data or findings?
15. Are you aware of other assessments or data that would help understand needs in this area?

### E: Red flags and concerns
16. Are there any protection concerns, political sensitivities, or access constraints in this area that a new actor should know about?
17. Are there any actors operating in this area whose activities raise concerns?

## Enumerator / facilitator guidance
Include local CBOs — they have critical knowledge that international NGOs may not. Request 5W data sharing. Note which organizations are willing vs. reluctant to coordinate.

## Data quality checks
- Minimum 5 organizations to constitute strong evidence for Domain 5 (coordination complexity)
- At least 2 CBOs required to assess local capacity (Domain 6)
- Cross-check coverage claims with observation

## Analysis use
Populate stakeholder 5W mapping form. Score Domains 5 (coordination complexity) and 6 (local capacity) in Tool #01. Identify potential coordination platform members.

## Risks / ethical safeguards
- Organizations may underreport activities due to competition for funding
- Do not share one organization's concerns about another without anonymization
- Be alert to actors who are antagonistic toward coordination
