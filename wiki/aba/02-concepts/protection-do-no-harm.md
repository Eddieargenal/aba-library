# Definition
The principle that humanitarian programs must avoid creating or exacerbating harm to affected populations, including through unintended negative consequences of geographic targeting, data collection, community engagement, resource allocation, or service delivery.

# Why it matters for urban DRR and response
Urban settings with diverse power dynamics, conflict history, or ethnically mixed populations require systematic protection analysis at every program stage — area-based targeting can create protection risks if boundary definition or beneficiary selection excludes or exposes vulnerable groups.
