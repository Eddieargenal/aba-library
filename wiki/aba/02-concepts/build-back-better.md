# Definition
The principle of using post-disaster recovery as an opportunity to reduce future risk, improve livelihoods, and build long-term resilience rather than simply restoring pre-disaster conditions that made communities vulnerable in the first place.

# Why it matters for urban DRR and response
Area-based programs in recovery contexts must integrate DRR principles into reconstruction — rebuilding to pre-crisis standards recreates risk and misses the leverage window that post-disaster political will provides for transformative change.
