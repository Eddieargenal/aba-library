# Definition
An analytical approach that treats the city as a system of interconnected infrastructure, services, governance, markets, land, mobility, and social networks, where change in one subsystem affects others. It looks for cascading effects, feedback loops, and leverage points rather than isolated problems.

# Why it matters for urban DRR and response
Urban DRR interventions that ignore systems interdependencies create unintended negative effects and miss leverage points. A WASH intervention that ignores the market system may fail to achieve sustainability; a shelter program that ignores tenure governance may create displacement risk.
