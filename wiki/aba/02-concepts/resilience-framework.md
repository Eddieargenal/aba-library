<a id="definition"></a>
## Definition

The "Characteristics of a Disaster-Resilient Community" describe an **ideal end-state** of resilience across thematic areas — an aspirational reference model, not an attainable checklist. No community can be made completely safe, and no single organisation can address every characteristic; coverage is necessarily partial and partnered.

<a id="field-use"></a>
## Field Use

Use the framework to orient strategy and surface gaps, then **scope ambition to mandate and capacity** — address a deliberate subset while partners or duty bearers cover the rest. Pairs with the caveat (finding F-003) that the characteristics must be adapted to local context, never applied as a 167-item checklist.

<a id="risks"></a>
## Risks

Treating the ideal state as a compliance target; small organisations over-reaching by attempting full coverage. See proposed risk **R-resilience-conflict-gap** for the conflict-setting limitation.
