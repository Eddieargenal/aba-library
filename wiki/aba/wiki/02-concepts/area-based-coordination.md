# Definition
A coordination mechanism that brings together humanitarian actors, local authorities, and service providers within a defined geographic area to prevent duplication, fill gaps, and ensure coherent multi-sector response. It operates at the area level rather than at the sector/cluster level.

# Why it matters for urban DRR and response
Without area-based coordination, multi-sector ABA programs risk duplication, missed gaps, and poor sequencing across actors. Area coordination complements cluster coordination and is especially critical in complex urban contexts with many actors covering overlapping geographies.
