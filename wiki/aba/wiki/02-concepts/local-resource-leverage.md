# Definition
The identification and mobilization of existing local capacities, actors, infrastructure, markets, and social networks to amplify humanitarian response outcomes, improve sustainability, and reduce dependency on external actors.

# Why it matters for urban DRR and response
Urban ABA programs that ignore local resources miss opportunities for sustainability, efficiency, and community ownership — local actors have context knowledge, existing relationships, and mandates that humanitarian actors cannot replicate.
