# Participatory Mapping Guide

## Purpose
A facilitation guide for community participatory mapping sessions where community members identify hazards, services, resources, boundaries, and priorities on a sketch map or satellite image of their area. Produces community-generated spatial knowledge that supplements or validates secondary data.

## When to use
- During area profile and systems diagnosis (Stage 2)
- During community engagement platform design (Stage 3)
- When validating proposed area boundaries with community members

## Who facilitates
Field staff with community facilitation skills; ideally bilingual; gender-balanced facilitation team recommended.

## Participants
Minimum 10 community members per session; must include women, youth, and other marginalized group representatives; conduct separate sessions if mixed groups create participation barriers.

## Required materials
- Large paper or printed satellite image (minimum A2/tabloid size)
- Colored markers (different colors for hazards, services, resources, boundaries)
- Sticky notes
- Consent form
- Photo camera

## Analysis use
Community maps are analyzed alongside secondary data to: validate area boundaries; identify hazard zones not in official data; locate services and resources; understand community priority areas.

## Risks / ethical safeguards
- Do not record names on maps that show protection-sensitive information
- Obtain informed consent before photographing maps
- Do not share maps showing beneficiary concentration with security actors
- Ensure participation is voluntary and no coercion
