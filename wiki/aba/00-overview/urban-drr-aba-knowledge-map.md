# Urban DRR + ABA Knowledge Map

This wiki organizes knowledge for AI agents and technical teams working on urban disaster risk reduction (DRR) and area-based approaches (ABA).

## What this wiki is for
Helping technical teams design, implement, and adapt urban emergency response strategies using area-based approaches, from initial appropriateness decisions through transition and handover.

## Knowledge layers

### Layer 1: Sources (./01-sources/extracted/)
Primary documents: academic papers, practitioner guides, toolkits, and frameworks. 18 sources cataloged.

### Layer 2: Concepts (./02-concepts/)
Core ideas: area-based approach, urban systems thinking, hazard-exposure-vulnerability-capacity, resilience, stakeholder mapping. 18 concept pages.

### Layer 3: Frameworks (./03-frameworks/)
Decision frameworks that structure how teams approach key choices: ABA appropriateness, area selection, neighborhood diagnosis, joint prioritization.

### Layer 4: Tools (./04-tools/)
17 operational tools that guide evidence collection, analysis, and decision-making across the 11 lifecycle stages. Each tool specifies evidence required, collection method, analysis, and scoring.

### Layer 5: Field Instruments (./05-field-instruments/)
18 data collection forms, checklists, and interview guides. Linked to tools.

### Layer 6: Lifecycle (./06-lifecycle/)
11 stages from appropriateness decision through transition and scaling.

### Layer 7: Sectors, Coordination, MEL, Transition (./07-sector-applications/ through ./10-transition-scale/)
Sector-specific guidance, coordination tools, monitoring frameworks, and handover plans.
