# Household Mini Survey

## Purpose
A brief 15-20 minute household survey administered to a non-random sample of households in the target area to collect household-level data on needs, vulnerabilities, service access, and risk perception. Supplements key informant interviews and observation with household-level evidence.

## When to use
Stage 0-1 (appropriateness / area selection) — administer to minimum 30 households across the area before scoring Tool #01.

## Who collects / facilitates
Trained enumerator, ideally from local community. Female enumerator required for female respondents where cultural norms require.

## Respondents / participants
Adult household head or representative. Target 50% female respondents. Minimum 30 households; aim for spatial spread across area zones.

## Questions / survey fields

**Enumerator instructions:** Read each question aloud. Do not suggest answers. Record responses verbatim where applicable. Skip sections that do not apply.

### Consent and identification
- Date / enumerator ID:
- Zone / block (do not record street address or name):
- Informed consent obtained: [ ] yes (proceed) [ ] no (stop, thank, leave)
- Respondent sex: [ ] F [ ] M [ ] other
- Respondent age: ___
- Household size: ___ (adults: ___ / children under 18: ___)

### Section A: Displacement and housing
- How long has this household lived here? [ ] < 6 months [ ] 6 mo–2 yr [ ] > 2 years
- Was the household displaced by the crisis? [ ] yes — from: ___ [ ] no
- Current housing type: [ ] owned permanent [ ] owned semi-permanent [ ] renting [ ] hosted [ ] shelter/camp [ ] other
- Housing damage: [ ] none [ ] minor (habitable) [ ] major (unsafe) [ ] destroyed
- Tenure security: [ ] formally owned/leased [ ] informal occupancy [ ] pending eviction risk [ ] other

### Section C: Vulnerability indicators
- Household members with disability: [ ] yes — type: ___ [ ] no
- Household members who are elderly (60+) and living alone/without support: [ ] yes [ ] no
- Female-headed household: [ ] yes [ ] no
- Chronic illness requiring regular medication/care: [ ] yes [ ] no

### Section D: Hazard and risk perception
- Is your household in a high-risk zone (flood, collapse, etc.)? [ ] yes — which: ___ [ ] no [ ] don't know
- Has your household experienced a hazard event in the past 12 months? [ ] yes — describe: ___ [ ] no

- Have you received any humanitarian assistance in the past 3 months? [ ] yes — what: ___ [ ] no
- Are there needs that no organization is addressing? [ ] yes — what: ___ [ ] no [ ] don't know

### Section F: Community participation
- Would you be willing to participate in community meetings about how assistance is designed? [ ] yes [ ] no [ ] maybe
- Are there groups in this community who tend to be left out of consultations? [ ] yes — who: ___ [ ] no [ ] don't know
- Who do community members trust to represent their interests? (open): ___

## Enumerator / facilitator guidance
Do not record household addresses or names. Administer across all area zones, not just accessible streets. Seek participation of female respondents, elderly, and persons with disability. Stop if respondent shows distress.

## Data quality checks
- Minimum 30 completed surveys before using for domain scoring
- At least 40% female respondents required for gender-disaggregated analysis
- Spatial spread: at least 3 different zones represented
- Do not use if fewer than 50% of target households completed all sections

## Analysis use
Aggregate and tabulate by section. Calculate % of HH reporting each need/service gap. Disaggregate by sex, displacement status, and housing type. Use to score Domains 2, 3, and 5 in Tool #01.

## Risks / ethical safeguards
- Do not collect names or addresses
- Store surveys in locked location; digitize and delete paper versions after verification
- Do not share household-level data with authorities or community leaders
- Ensure respondent knows participation is voluntary and refusal has no consequences
