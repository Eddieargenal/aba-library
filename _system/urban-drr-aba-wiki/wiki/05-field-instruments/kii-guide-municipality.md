# Kii Guide Municipality

## Purpose
A key informant interview guide for municipal authorities and local government officials. Collects data on governance capacity, local plans, resource allocation, coordination willingness, existing systems, and political context for ABA feasibility assessment.

## When to use
Stage 0-1 (appropriateness decision and area selection). Conduct before finalizing area boundaries.

## Who collects / facilitates
Senior program staff or team leader. Bilingual. Should be conducted by staff with authority to engage government counterparts.

## Respondents / participants
Municipal mayor or deputy; urban planning director; disaster risk management focal point; ward/district administrator. Minimum 2 different municipal officials.

## Questions / interview guide

**Interviewer instructions:** This is a semi-structured guide. Use questions as prompts, not a rigid script. Probe for specifics. Record notes verbatim where possible. Duration: 45-60 minutes.

### Consent and introduction
- Introduce organization, purpose of assessment, how data will be used
- Confirm: notes will be used internally; no direct attribution without permission
- Confirm consent to proceed

### A: Understanding the crisis and area
1. How does the municipality officially characterize this crisis — disaster, displacement, infrastructure failure, or other?
2. Which neighborhoods/zones have been officially designated as affected areas?
3. What is the municipal damage and needs estimate for [target area]? What is the evidence base?
4. Are there areas within [zone] that municipal authorities have formally declared as priority for response?

### B: Existing plans and coordination
5. Does the municipality have a disaster risk management plan or local resilience plan? What does it say about [target area]?
6. Is there an existing municipal coordination platform for this crisis? Who leads it? Which actors participate?
7. Is the municipality coordinating with UN agencies, NGOs, or other external actors? How — formally or informally?
8. Who is the designated municipal counterpart for humanitarian coordination?

### C: Municipal capacity and resources
9. What municipal services are currently functioning in [target area] — water, sanitation, electricity, roads, health, schools?
10. What resources has the municipality allocated to [target area] response? What is the budget? What is the timeline?
11. What is the biggest constraint on municipal response capacity — resources, staff, systems, political?
12. Are there municipal service providers (utilities, waste, housing authority) that are engaged in or relevant to the response?

### D: Area-based approach feasibility
13. Would the municipality support an external organization conducting a coordinated multi-sector program in [target area]?
14. Are there any restrictions on external organization presence or activities in [target area]?
15. Are there areas within [target area] that external actors cannot or should not access?
16. Would the municipality participate in an area coordination platform convened by [organization] or a lead actor?

### E: Land, tenure, and protection
17. What is the land tenure status of [target area] — formal titles, informal occupancy, disputed?
18. Are there any eviction orders, demolition plans, or resettlement plans affecting [target area]?
19. Are there specific population groups in [target area] that the municipality considers a political or security concern?

### F: Priorities and handover
20. What does the municipality most need from external organizations in [target area]?
21. What would a successful program handover to the municipality look like from your perspective?
22. What would need to be in place for the municipality to absorb program functions after external actors exit?

### Closing
- Confirm follow-up contact for coordination
- Request any documents: maps, plans, service data, budget documents

## Enumerator / facilitator guidance
Conduct in a formal setting if possible. Bring organizational letter of introduction. Do not pressure for answer on cooperation before building relationship. Follow up with documents request in writing.

## Data quality checks
- Must be conducted with minimum 2 different officials at different levels
- Do not use single municipal source as sole basis for any domain score
- Cross-check all municipal capacity claims with observation and service provider interviews

## Analysis use
Populate Domain 5 (coordination complexity), Domain 6 (local capacity), and Domain 9 (operational feasibility) in Tool #01. Flag any red flags about access, authority refusal, or political risk.

## Risks / ethical safeguards
- Do not share community-identified protection concerns with municipal authorities without prior consent
- Be alert to officials who may use information for political purposes
- Distinguish between official statements and operational reality
