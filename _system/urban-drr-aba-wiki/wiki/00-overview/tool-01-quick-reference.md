# Tool #01 Quick Reference

> Compact scoring model and core decision rules. For full evidence collection procedures, field instruments, and module detail → [[wiki/04-tools/01-aba-feasibility-and-necessity-assessment-tool]]

---

## What This Tool Decides

Whether an Area-Based Approach is **feasible, necessary, and appropriate** for a given context.

**Use it when:** You are deciding whether to design a program as an ABA vs. a sector-specific or household-level intervention.

**Minimum pre-conditions before running:** You must have geographic concentration data, at least one KII with municipal or community actors, and a preliminary 5W actor mapping.

---

## Scoring Model — 8 Domains

| # | Domain | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---:|---|
| 1 | Geographic concentration | 15% | % of affected population in target area vs. wider zone |
| 2 | Multi-sector need | 15% | Number of interdependent failing sectors |
| 3 | Urban systems interdependence | 15% | Degree to which system failures are causing sector failures |
| 4 | Stakeholder/coordination complexity | 15% | Number of actors, duplication risk, gap risk |
| 5 | Local capacity/resource leverage | 10% | Existing local capacity that ABA can build on |
| 6 | DRR/future risk relevance | 15% | Degree to which hazard/exposure/vulnerability drives the situation |
| 7 | Participation feasibility | 10% | Whether meaningful participation is achievable in this timeline |
| 8 | Operational feasibility/safeguards | 5% | Access, protection risks, program viability |

**Scoring formula:** For each domain → `(raw score 0–4) ÷ 4 × weight × 100`

**Total score:** Sum all 8 domain scores (max = 100)

---

## Decision Categories

| Score | Decision |
|---:|---|
| 80–100 | Full ABA justified — proceed with full multi-sector, participatory area design |
| 65–79 | ABA justified with conditions — address specific domain weaknesses before launching |
| 50–64 | Hybrid model — lead with 1–2 sectors, build toward ABA if conditions improve |
| 35–49 | Sector or household approach preferred — ABA overhead not justified |
| 0–34 | Do not use ABA — conditions not met; risk of harm from forcing the model |

---

## Five Core Screening Questions

Before running the full tool, screen with these 5 questions. **If ≤ 1 yes → do not run full tool.**

1. Are needs geographically concentrated? (>40% of most-affected population in one localized area)
2. Are 3+ sectors failing in interdependent ways? (failure in one sector worsening another)
3. Are urban systems driving the problem? (water, markets, transport, services, land)
4. Are 4+ actors present with uncoordinated activities?
5. Is meaningful community participation feasible within this timeline?

**≥ 3 yes → run full tool**
**2 yes → judgment call; document why you ran or didn't run**
**≤ 1 yes → document rationale for not using ABA; consider sector approach**

## Required Outputs

The tool produces 12 outputs. **Minimum required for decision memo:**

1. Domain scores and total score
2. Decision category with rationale
3. Conditions or caveats (for 65–79 scores)
4. Recommended next step (proceed / conditional / hybrid / don't proceed)
5. Signed decision memo → [[wiki/05-field-instruments/decision-memo-template]]

---

## Key Risks to Check Before Scoring

- Domain 7 (Participation) score < 2 → ABA not viable regardless of total score
- Domain 8 (Operational feasibility) score < 1 → flag access or protection barriers before proceeding
- Any domain with data quality flag → note uncertainty in memo; do not treat score as definitive
