# Common ABA Misuse Patterns

## Purpose

Documents recurring patterns where area-based approaches are applied incorrectly, superficially, or in ways that harm target populations. Enables agents to flag risk when a proposed programme design matches a known misuse pattern.

## Misuse Pattern Registry

### Pattern 1: Geographic Box-Drawing Without Analysis
Selecting an area based on administrative boundaries or programme convenience rather than vulnerability analysis. Leads to inclusion of low-need households and exclusion of high-need ones at the margins.

### Pattern 2: Area-Based Label on Household-Based Programming
Calling a programme "area-based" while implementing sector-by-sector household targeting. Loses the systems-thinking and coordination benefits of genuine ABA.

### Pattern 3: Coordination Theatre
Creating area-based coordination meetings that produce minutes but no decisions or accountability. Consumes actor time without reducing overlap or improving coherence.

### Pattern 4: Premature Exit
Handing over to local government or declaring success before target outcomes are achieved or local capacity is confirmed. Often driven by funding cycles.

### Pattern 5: Participation Washing
Conducting one-off community consultations and claiming participatory design without ongoing community voice in decisions.
