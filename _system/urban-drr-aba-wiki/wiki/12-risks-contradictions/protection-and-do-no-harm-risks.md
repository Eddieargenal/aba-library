# Protection and Do-No-Harm Risks in Urban ABA

## Purpose

Catalogues protection risks specific to area-based approaches in urban settings. These risks arise from the intersection of geographic targeting, community mapping, coordination data sharing, and engagement with local power structures. Field teams must screen against these risks before and during programme implementation.

### Data-Related Risks
- **Community mapping exposure**: Participatory mapping revealing household locations of vulnerable groups (IDPs, minority groups, LGBTQ+ individuals) to hostile actors
- **Beneficiary list misuse**: Area-targeting data repurposed for discriminatory local governance
- **Coordination data sharing**: Sensitive population data shared in multi-actor coordination without data protection protocols

### Targeting Risks
- **Elite capture**: Local leaders influencing area selection or within-area targeting to benefit connected households
- **Exclusion by design**: Area boundaries that systematically exclude informal settlements or minority neighbourhoods
- **Stigmatisation**: Area labelling as "crisis zone" affecting property values or social status of residents

### Engagement Risks
- **Armed actor engagement**: Legitimate local structure engagement that inadvertently legitimises armed or criminal groups
- **Gender-based harm**: Participation structures that increase women's visibility and exposure to intimate partner violence
- **Community tension**: Resource-based conflict between targeted and non-targeted areas or populations
