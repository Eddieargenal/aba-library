# Summary

This ALNAP case study documents People in Need (PCI)'s neighbourhood-based programming approach in Guatemala City (Barrio Mio program) and Port-au-Prince (Katye), two major informal urban settlement contexts. It provides empirical evidence of how neighbourhood-level approaches function in practice, including participatory engagement models, multi-sector integration, coordination with local actors, and adaptation to urban informality. The case study is a valuable empirical reference for validating ABA design principles in urban Latin American/Caribbean contexts.
