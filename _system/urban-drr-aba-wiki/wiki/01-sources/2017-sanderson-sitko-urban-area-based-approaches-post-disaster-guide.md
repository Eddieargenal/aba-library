# Summary

Sanderson and Sitko's 2017 IIED guide is the core operational reference for urban area-based approaches, providing detailed practical guidance across the full ABA lifecycle from appropriateness decision through transition. It introduces a suitability checklist grounded in geographic concentration, multi-sector needs, and local stakeholder engagement criteria; presents area selection and boundary definition methods; covers participatory planning, prioritization, implementation sequencing, MEL, and handover. This source is referenced across more matrix rows (8) than any other document in the wiki.

# Project lifecycle relevance

- **Stage 0 — Appropriateness decision**: Suitability checklist — the primary instrument for determining whether ABA is justified.
- **Stage 1 — Area selection**: Area selection matrix with hazard, vulnerability, feasibility, and strategic criteria.
- **Stage 3 — Community engagement**: Participatory assessment and planning methodology.
- **Stage 4 — Joint prioritization**: Severity-risk-feasibility matrix for needs prioritization.
- **Stage 5 — Integrated area strategy**: Area theory of change and results framework.
- **Stage 8 — Implementation**: Implementation sequencing and dependency mapping.
- **Stage 9 — MEL**: Area-based MEL framework design.
- **Stage 10 — Transition**: Handover readiness criteria and checklist.
