# Definition
The systematic identification and analysis of all actors (government, NGOs, CBOs, private sector, community) with roles, interests, resources, and power relationships relevant to an area-based program.

# Why it matters for urban DRR and response
Understanding stakeholder power dynamics is essential for avoiding duplication, leveraging local capacity, aligning with authorities, and managing coordination risks — ignoring power dynamics leads to programs that reproduce existing inequalities.
