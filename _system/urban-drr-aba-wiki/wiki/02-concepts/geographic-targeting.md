# Definition
The deliberate selection of specific geographic areas for humanitarian response based on need, vulnerability, concentration, and feasibility criteria, as distinct from population-based or institution-based targeting approaches.

# Why it matters for urban DRR and response
Appropriate geographic targeting ensures resources reach the most vulnerable areas while avoiding duplication and maintaining operational coherence — poor targeting wastes resources and excludes the most affected populations.
