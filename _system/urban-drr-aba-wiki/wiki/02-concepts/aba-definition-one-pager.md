# Area-Based Approach — Definition One-Pager

> Self-contained. Read this page to know what ABA is, what it isn't, and how to push back on misuse. No other pages needed.

---

## What Is an Area-Based Approach?

An Area-Based Approach (ABA) is a way of structuring humanitarian or development programming that meets **three conditions simultaneously**:

| Condition | What it means | What it is NOT |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Geographically targeted** | Area selected based on concentration of need, vulnerability, or hazard — not administrative convenience | Any program physically located in a neighborhood |
| **2. Multi-sector** | Addresses multiple interdependent sector needs in an integrated, coordinated way | Parallel single-sector programs in the same location |
| **3. Participatory** | Community has meaningful role in assessment, design, prioritization, and accountability | Informing community of decisions already made |

**All three must be present. Any two without the third is not an ABA.**

---

## Minimum Distinguishing Features

An ABA program must have:
- An area selected through a documented needs/vulnerability analysis (not assigned by default)
- A coordination mechanism linking at least 3 sectors
- A community engagement structure with documented input into design decisions
- A shared area profile used by all actors in the area

---

## What ABA Is NOT

| Common claim | Why it fails the standard |
|---|---|
| "We work in one neighborhood, so we're area-based" | Geographic location alone does not meet any of the three criteria |
| "We coordinate with two other NGOs on referrals" | Referral coordination ≠ multi-sector integrated design |
| "We held a community meeting before starting" | Single consultation ≠ participatory design |
| "We selected the worst-affected district" | Administrative selection without multi-sector integration = targeted geographic program, not ABA |
| "We cover shelter AND NFI" | Two components of one sector = single sector program |

---

## How to Push Back on Misuse

When a program is described as "area-based," ask three questions:

**Q1 — Area selection:**
> "What vulnerability or risk data was used to select this specific area over adjacent areas? Show the criteria."
> If the answer is "it's our operational zone" or "the cluster assigned it" → not area-based.

**Q2 — Multi-sector integration:**
> "Which 3+ sectors are being addressed, and what is the coordination mechanism that links their design decisions?"
> If each sector is designed independently → not area-based.

**Q3 — Community participation:**
> "At what point did community input change the design of the program? What was proposed, what was changed, and who made that decision?"
> If the answer describes informing not deciding → not area-based.

---

## ABA Appropriateness — Quick Check

ABA is justified when all of the following are true:
1. Needs are geographically concentrated (>40% of affected population in one localized area)
2. Three or more sectors are failing in interdependent ways
3. Urban systems (water, markets, transport, services) are driving the problem
4. Four or more actors are present without coordinated activity
5. Community participation is feasible within the operational timeline

If two or more of these are false → consider sector-only or household approach.
